About
-----

I am an MD/PhD physician-scientist specializing in clinical hematology and computational biology. I currently work as a postdoctoral fellow jointly mentored by `Dr. Ton Schumacher <https://www.nki.nl/research/research-groups/ton-schumacher>`_ at the Netherlands Cancer Institute and `Dr. Dan Landau <https://www.landaulab.org/>`_ at Weill Cornell Medicine and the New York Genome Center, and as a specializing physician at Helsinki University Hospital's Comprehensive Cancer Center.

My expertise bridges clinical medicine and advanced computational analysis, with deep experience in:

- Single-cell RNA sequencing and multi-omics data analysis
- T cell receptor (TCR) repertoire analysis and antigen specificity prediction
- Cancer immunotherapy (checkpoint inhibitors, CAR-T, TKIs)
- Hematological malignancies (CML, AML, T-LGLL, aplastic anemia, melanoma)
- Machine learning applications in immunology and oncology

I have authored 25+ peer-reviewed publications (H-index 21, 1,500+ citations) in journals including *Nature Communications*, *Journal of Clinical Investigation*, *Immunity*, and *Blood*. I have developed open-source computational tools for TCR specificity prediction and maintain active repositories on GitHub.

My research training includes visiting positions at Stanford University (Mark Davis lab) and the Wellcome Sanger Institute (Sarah Teichmann lab), providing exposure to world-leading immunology and single-cell genomics methods. I have also provided scientific consulting for 5+ pharmaceutical companies in oncology, immunology, and autoimmune disease therapeutics.
